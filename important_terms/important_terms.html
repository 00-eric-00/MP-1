Important Terms

Resistive load

Loads that only comsumes active power and where current and voltage are in phase with each other. Examples: incandescent lighting or heating loads.

Inductive Load

Load which only consumes reactive power and where the current waveforms lags the voltage by 90 degree. Examples: Motors, Fans or anything that has a motor inside it.

Capacitive Load

Load which only consumes reactive power and where the current waveforms leads the voltage by 90 degree. Capacitive loads are installed to improve the power factor of a load.

Safety Factor

The load-carrying capacity of a system beyond what the system actually supports under a certain period of time.

Power Factor

The ratio of working power, measured in kilowatts (kW), to apparent power, measured in kilovolt amperes (kVA).

Branch Circuit

Any circuit that extends beyond the final overcurrent protective device is called a branch circuit.

Schedule of loads

Summary of data to easily identify and facilitate the necessary values and equipment rating to be used in any electrical installation.
